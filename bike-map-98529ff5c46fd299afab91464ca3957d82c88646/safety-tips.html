Cyclist Safety Kingston

Here are some useful tips to keep in mind when biking!

Always wear a helmet!

Statistics show that only 17% of bicycle related fatalities occured with people wearing helmets while 54% occured without a helmet

Always follow the rules of the road!

Cyclists are considered a vehicle on the road and follow all traffic laws. They are expected to behave like motor vehicles, stop at stop signs and red lights.

Be predictable!

Always follow the flow of traffic, motor vehicle drivers are not always expecting cyclists on the road. When unexpected, motor vehicles may intersect with your path and cause a crash.

Always be visible!

Sometimes it may be hard for drivers to spot bikes on the road. This is especially true at night. Wear bright /neon colors so that you are more visible. Having reflective strips and lights on your bike help a lot as well.

Look before turning!

when turning left or right, always look behind you for a break in traffic, then signal before making the turn. Watch for left- or right-turning traffic.

Yield to Traffic when appropriate!

drivers on a smaller road must yield (wait) for traffic on a major or larger road. If there is no stop sign or traffic signal and you are coming from a smaller roadway , you must slow down and look to see if the way is clear before proceeding.
